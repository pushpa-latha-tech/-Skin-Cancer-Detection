Precautions & Care

Essential guidelines for skin cancer prevention and early detection

Important Medical Notice

These guidelines are for educational purposes only and do not replace professional medical advice. If you notice any changes in your skin, unusual growths, or suspicious lesions, consult a qualified dermatologist immediately.

Sun Protection Guidelines

Avoid Excessive Sun Exposure

  • Peak Hours: Limit outdoor activities between 10 AM - 4 PM when UV rays are strongest
  • Shade Seeking: Stay under trees, umbrellas, or covered areas when outdoors
  • Weather Awareness: UV rays can penetrate clouds - protection needed even on cloudy days
  • Reflection Risk: Be extra careful around water, sand, and snow that reflect UV rays
  • Altitude Consideration: UV exposure increases with altitude - take extra precautions

Daily Sunscreen Protection

  • SPF 30 Minimum: Use broad-spectrum sunscreen with at least SPF 30
  • Generous Application: Apply 1 ounce (30ml) to cover entire body
  • Reapplication: Every 2 hours and after swimming or sweating
  • Year-Round Use: Apply sunscreen daily, even in winter months
  • Face Focus: Use separate facial sunscreen for sensitive facial skin

Protective Clothing & Accessories

Essential Protective Wear

Wide-Brimmed Hat

4-inch brim minimum to protect face, ears, and neck

UV-Blocking Sunglasses

100% UV protection to prevent eye and eyelid damage

Long-Sleeved Clothing

Tightly woven, dark-colored fabrics for maximum protection

Fabric Guidelines

Best Fabric Choices

  • Polyester and polyester blends
  • Tightly woven cotton
  • Denim and canvas materials
  • UPF-rated clothing (UPF 30+)

Avoid These Fabrics

  • Loosely woven cotton
  • Thin or lightweight materials
  • White or light-colored fabrics
  • Wet clothing (reduces protection)

Regular Self-Examination

Monthly Skin Checks

Perform a thorough self-examination every month to detect changes early

ABCDE Method

Look for these warning signs in moles and lesions:

A

Asymmetry

One half doesn't match the other

B

Border

Irregular, scalloped, or poorly defined edges

C

Color

Multiple colors or uneven color distribution

D

Diameter

Larger than 6mm (pencil eraser size)

E

Evolving

Changes in size, shape, color, or texture

Self-Exam Steps

  1. Full Body Mirror: Examine front and back in full-length mirror
  2. Arms & Hands: Check underarms, forearms, palms, and between fingers
  3. Legs & Feet: Sit down to examine legs, feet, and between toes
  4. Hair & Scalp: Use hand mirror or ask partner to check scalp
  5. Face & Neck: Carefully examine all areas including behind ears
  6. Documentation: Take photos of suspicious spots to track changes

Pro Tip

Use a handheld mirror and good lighting to check hard-to-see areas. Consider having a partner help with areas you can't see clearly.

Professional Healthcare

Regular Dermatology Checkups

  • Annual Screening: Full-body skin exam by dermatologist once yearly
  • High-Risk Individuals: More frequent checkups if you have risk factors
  • Family History: Earlier and more frequent screenings if melanoma runs in family
  • Previous Skin Cancer: Regular follow-up appointments as recommended
  • Suspicious Changes: Immediate appointment for any concerning lesions

When to See a Doctor Immediately

Urgent Warning Signs

  • New mole appearing after age 30
  • Existing mole changing rapidly
  • Mole that bleeds, itches, or becomes painful
  • Sore that doesn't heal within 4 weeks
  • Any spot that looks different from others

Additional Risk Reduction

Avoid Tanning

  • No Tanning Beds: Artificial UV radiation significantly increases cancer risk
  • No Sunlamps: Avoid all artificial tanning devices
  • Embrace Natural Tone: Your natural skin tone is beautiful and safe
  • Safe Alternatives: Use sunless tanning products if desired

Healthy Lifestyle

  • Immune Support: Maintain strong immune system through healthy diet
  • Avoid Smoking: Smoking increases skin cancer risk
  • Stay Hydrated: Keep skin healthy with adequate water intake
  • Stress Management: Chronic stress can weaken immune function
