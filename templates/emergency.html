When to Seek Immediate Medical Attention

ðŸš¨ Urgent Skin Cancer Signs

  • Rapidly changing mole - size, shape, or color changes within weeks
  • Bleeding lesion - any skin spot that bleeds without trauma
  • Non-healing sore - doesn't heal after 4 weeks
  • New pigmented lesion after age 30
  • Painful or itchy mole that wasn't before
  • Irregular borders that are becoming more jagged

âš¡ Other Emergency Signs

  • Severe allergic reaction - swelling, difficulty breathing
  • Signs of infection - pus, red streaking, fever
  • Severe burns - extensive sun damage or chemical burns
  • Suspicious lymph nodes - swollen, hard, or painful
  • Systemic symptoms - fever, fatigue with skin changes
  • Multiple new lesions appearing rapidly

Don't Wait - Act Fast

Early intervention can be life-saving. Trust your instincts - if something feels wrong, seek medical attention immediately.

Preparing for Your Medical Visit

Before Your Appointment

  • Document symptoms: Take photos of concerning lesions
  • List questions: Write down what you want to ask
  • Medical history: Gather family history information
  • Current medications: Bring list of all medications
  • Insurance cards: Verify coverage and bring ID
  • Previous results: Bring any prior test results

Questions to Ask

  • What type of skin lesion/cancer is this?
  • What stage is it, if malignant?
  • What are my treatment options?
  • What are the risks and benefits of each option?
  • How often should I have follow-up exams?
  • Are there lifestyle changes I should make?
